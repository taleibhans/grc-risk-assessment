# **LuxeOnGo — Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)**

**Version 1.0**
**Effective Date:** January 2025

---

# **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to establish clear expectations for the responsible, secure, and ethical use of LuxeOnGo’s information systems, technology resources, cloud platforms, data, and devices.
This policy ensures that all users:

* Protect customer information and company data
* Maintain uptime, integrity, and confidentiality of systems
* Use technology only for acceptable and authorized business purposes
* Understand prohibited behaviors that create risk

This AUP supports LuxeOnGo’s broader commitment to information security, risk management, and compliance with ISO 27001, NIST CSF, and relevant privacy regulations.

---

# **2. Scope**

This policy applies to:

### **2.1 Users Covered**

* All LuxeOnGo employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
* Contractors, consultants, and vendors with system access
* Partners or third parties accessing LuxeOnGo assets
* Interns and trainees

### **2.2 Assets Covered**

This policy governs the use of:

* Employee laptops, POS tablets, and mobile devices
* Cloud services (AWS, Google Workspace, CRM, IMS)
* Corporate email accounts
* Vending machine POS technology
* Network resources (Wi-Fi, VPN, routers, firewalls)
* Shared drives and digital storage
* Customer, product, financial, and operational data

### **2.3 Activities Covered**

Includes both:

* On-premises activities (offices, vending sites)
* Remote work activities (home networks, public Wi-Fi)

---

# **3. Roles & Responsibilities**

### **3.1 Users**

All users are responsible for:

* Following this AUP at all times
* Protecting credentials and devices
* Reporting suspicious activity immediately
* Using systems only for authorized business purposes

### **3.2 IT Department**

Responsible for:

* System access provisioning and deprovisioning
* Monitoring system and network usage
* Detecting and responding to security incidents
* Enforcing compliance with the AUP

### **3.3 Management**

Responsible for:

* Approving access requests
* Ensuring employees complete required training
* Supporting enforcement actions when non-compliance occurs

---

# **4. Acceptable Use Requirements**

Users must follow all of the following rules at all times:

### **4.1 Access Control**

* Use only the accounts assigned to you
* Always enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Do not share passwords with anyone
* Lock laptops and tablets when unattended

### **4.2 Data Handling**

* Store sensitive information only in approved cloud systems
* Encrypt data in transit and at rest where applicable
* Dispose of sensitive data according to retention guidelines
* Do not email customer or financial data unencrypted

### **4.3 Device Usage**

* Only install approved applications
* Maintain updated antivirus/EDR software
* Connect only to secure Wi-Fi networks or use the company VPN
* Report stolen or lost devices immediately

### **4.4 Internet & Email**

* Use company email for business-related communication
* Do not access malicious or suspicious websites
* Do not open unsolicited attachments from unknown senders
* Do not sign up for external services using corporate email unless authorized

### **4.5 Cloud Platform Usage**

For systems like AWS, Google Workspace, CRM, IMS:

* Follow role-based access restrictions
* Do not bypass security configurations
* Do not modify system settings without approval

### **4.6 Physical Security**

* Keep POS tablets secure during vending operations
* Do not leave devices in public areas
* Follow all access badge and visitor procedures

---

# **5. Prohibited Activities**

The following behaviors are strictly **prohibited** as they create security, legal, or operational risks.

### **5.1 Unauthorized Access**

* Attempting to bypass authentication or security controls
* Using another employee’s login credentials
* Accessing data, systems, or areas without authorization

### **5.2 Malicious or Inappropriate Behavior**

* Introducing malware, ransomware, or unauthorized software
* Conducting penetration testing without written approval
* Sending threatening, discriminatory, or inappropriate emails
* Downloading copyrighted content illegally

### **5.3 Misuse of Technology Resources**

* Using corporate systems for personal financial gain
* Excessive streaming or personal cloud storage use
* Running cryptocurrency mining or unauthorized servers

### **5.4 Data Misuse**

* Sharing LuxeOnGo data with unauthorized third parties
* Exporting customer, employee, or transaction data without approval
* Removing company data from devices during termination

### **5.5 Physical Device Misuse**

* Altering vending machine configurations
* Tampering with POS tablets or security locks
* Using unapproved USB devices

---

# **6. Monitoring & Logging**

LuxeOnGo reserves the right to **monitor, log, and review**:

* Email communications
* File access logs
* Network activity
* Cloud platform usage
* Device security events

Monitoring is performed for:

* Security
* Compliance
* Operational continuity

Users should have **no expectation of privacy** on company systems, except where required by law.

---

# **7. Incident Reporting Requirements**

Users must report the following **immediately** to IT Security:

* Lost or stolen laptops, tablets, or credentials
* Suspicious emails or phishing attempts
* Malware alerts or unusual system behavior
* Unauthorized access or policy violations
* Misconfigured cloud shares or exposed data

Reports may be submitted via:

* Email to **[security@luxeongo.ca](mailto:security@luxeongo.ca)**
* Internal helpdesk ticket
* Direct escalation to the IT Administrator

---

# **8. Enforcement & Consequences**

Violations of this policy may result in:

* Mandatory security retraining
* Temporary account suspension
* Loss of system privileges
* Formal disciplinary action
* Termination of employment or contract
* Legal action where applicable

Severity is based on:

* Intent (accidental vs. malicious)
* Damage or exposure caused
* Recurrence of violations

---

# **9. Policy Review & Maintenance**

This policy will be reviewed:

* Annually
* After major system or organizational changes
* After a significant security incident

Policy updates will be communicated to all users and require acknowledgment.

## **Appendix A — Definitions**

**Acceptable Use:**
Proper, authorized, and safe use of company systems.

**Confidential Data:**
Information that could cause harm if disclosed (customer data, financial records, employee PII).

**EDR:**
Endpoint Detection & Response security software.

**MFA:**
An authentication method requiring two or more verification factors.

**PII:**
Personally Identifiable Information.

**POS Device:**
Point-of-Sale tablet used at LuxeOnGo vending machines.

**Sensitive Information:**
Any data with a classification of Medium or High in LuxeOnGo's Data Handling Policy.
